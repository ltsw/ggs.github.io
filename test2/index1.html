What is National Learn To Swim Week?

Grenada is an island country in the Caribbean about 100 miles north of Venezuela. Like other Caribbean countries many National Grenadians, children and adults, cannot swim (estimated at over 90%) resulting in unfortunate drownings.

Spearheaded by Deb Eastwood and the Grenada Youth Adventurers (GYA), National Learn to Swim Week (NLTSW) is an initiative to provide free swim lessons to children and adults throughout Grenada. The pilot program launched over the Easter of 2015 welcomed over 500 new swimmers (100 of them adults) at 11 different beaches and pools.

About NLTSW

NLTSW 2015 was an unprecedented success with over 100 volunteers and the support of sponsors such as Republic Bank Ltd, the Grenada Olympic Committee and various local businesses and marinas. Endorsed by the Government of Grenada's Ministry of Sport and the Grenada Amateur Swimming Association (GASA), National Learn To Swim Week looks to become an integral part of water safety and learn-to-swim each year.

VOLUNTEER

You do not have to be a great swimmer to be NLTSW Volunteer. As long as you are comfortable in the water, it will only take an hour for us to train you how to work with the children (and adults!). They need to be able to blow bubbles underwater, float on their bellies and backs, kick with a noodle...simple stuff like that.
