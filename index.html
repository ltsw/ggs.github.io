What is National Learn To Swim Week?

Grenada is an island country in the Caribbean about 100 miles north of Venezuela. Like other Caribbean countries many National Grenadians, children and adults, cannot swim (estimated at over 90%) resulting in unfortunate drownings.

Spearheaded by Deb Eastwood and the Grenada Youth Adventurers (GYA), National Learn to Swim Week (NLTSW) is an initiative to provide free swim lessons to children and adults throughout Grenada. The pilot program launched over the Easter of 2015 welcomed over 500 new swimmers, 100 of them adults, at 11 different beaches and pools.

About NLTSW

NLTSW 2015 was an unprecedented success with over 100 volunteers and the support of sponsors such as Republic Bank Ltd, the Grenada Olympic Committee and various local businesses and marinas.

Endorsed by the Government of Grenada's Ministry of Sport and the Grenada Amateur Swimming Association (GASA), National Learn To Swim Week looks to become an integral part of water safety and learn-to-swim each year.

The NLTSW Organisation is a not-for-profit organisation, funded by the kind sponsorship of companies and donations from wonderful people like yourself.

About Grenada Youth Adventurers

Grenada Youth Adventurers (GYA) Purpose: To provide inspiring recreational, leadership, service, educational, and social activities for Grenada youth and families which nurture a sense of community, that build character, teach leadership skills, and inspire a service ethic. Working with schools and community organizations, GYA helps youth in these communities realize their true potential through an emphasis on swimming, outdoor adventure programs, trips, and camps. About NLTSW Co-Ordinator Deb Eastwood

Deb Eastwood came to Grenada in 2007 after 4 years of service in the US Air Force, a business degree from the University of North Dakota, 20 years in sales at the Xerox Corporation, and 10 years of work with non-profits. She has been working with youth for over 30 years as a mentor, coach, youth leader, Sunday School teacher and camp director.

Deb was a full time teacher on the island for four years at several Grenadian schools and for the past 4 years has founded and directed a non-profit youth organization called GYA (Grenada Youth Adventurers) which has a heavy focus on swimming. Deb is a FINA Level 2 coach, a current certified life guard and an American Red Cross lifeguard instructor who certifies other lifeguards in Grenada.
